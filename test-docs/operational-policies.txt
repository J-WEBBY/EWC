University of Oldbury Students' Guild — Operational Policies & Procedures Manual
Version 3.2 — Effective September 2025

SECTION 1: GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

1.1 Decision Authority Matrix
- Expenditure up to £500: Department head approval
- Expenditure £500-£5,000: President + Finance Director dual approval
- Expenditure £5,000-£25,000: Executive Committee approval (quorum: 3 of 4 officers)
- Expenditure over £25,000: Board of Trustees approval
- Emergency expenditure: President may authorise up to £10,000 with retrospective Board ratification within 14 days

1.2 Meeting Cadence
- Executive Committee: Weekly (Monday 10am)
- Senior Management Team: Fortnightly (Wednesday 2pm)
- Board of Trustees: Quarterly + AGM in November
- Student Council: Monthly during term time (first Thursday, 6pm)

SECTION 2: FINANCIAL PROCEDURES

2.1 Budget Management
- Annual budget cycle: Draft by May, Board approval by July, operational from August 1
- Monthly management accounts produced by 15th of following month
- Quarterly variance analysis presented to Executive Committee
- All budget holders must flag variances exceeding 10% within 5 working days

2.2 Procurement
- Purchases over £1,000 require minimum 3 quotes
- Purchases over £10,000 require formal tender process (minimum 21 days)
- Preferred supplier list reviewed annually by Finance Director
- All contracts over £5,000 reviewed by Guild solicitor before signing

2.3 Cash Handling
- Bar and shop tills reconciled daily by shift supervisor
- Cash banked within 2 working days (max holding: £2,000 in safe)
- Till discrepancies over £20 investigated and reported to Commercial Director
- Card payments preferred — Guild targeting 90% cashless by 2026

SECTION 3: HUMAN RESOURCES

3.1 Staffing
- 28 permanent staff, 45 casual/student staff
- Student staff: minimum National Living Wage, maximum 15 hours/week during term
- All staff: enhanced DBS check required
- Probation period: 3 months (permanent), 4 weeks (casual)

3.2 Sabbatical Officers
- Four elected positions: President, VP Education, VP Welfare, VP Activities
- Term: 12 months (July 1 to June 30)
- Salary: £22,500 (reviewed annually, pegged to NUS recommended rate)
- Handover period: 2 weeks overlap with outgoing officers
- Mandatory training: NUS Lead & Change programme, trustee duties, media training

3.3 Performance Management
- Annual appraisals for all permanent staff (April-May cycle)
- 360-degree feedback for sabbatical officers at 6-month mark
- Capability procedure: informal discussion → formal improvement plan → final review
- Disciplinary procedure follows ACAS Code of Practice

SECTION 4: HEALTH & SAFETY

4.1 Event Safety
- All events with 100+ attendees require formal risk assessment (template RA-001)
- Licensed capacity: Main Hall 800, Bar 250, Meeting Rooms 30-60
- First aiders required: 1 per 100 attendees
- SIA-licensed security required for all evening events selling alcohol
- External event organisers must provide £5M public liability insurance

4.2 Building Operations
- Building open hours: Monday-Friday 8am-11pm, Saturday 10am-11pm, Sunday closed (except events)
- Fire evacuation drill: once per term, coordinated with university fire officer
- CCTV: 24 cameras, 30-day retention, managed by Estates department
- Lone working policy: No staff member alone in building after 9pm

SECTION 5: DATA PROTECTION (GDPR)

5.1 Data Controller: Students' Guild (separate from university)
5.2 DPO: Sarah Thompson (interim, pending appointment of dedicated DPO)
5.3 Student data: Membership records, society memberships, election data, welfare case files
5.4 Retention: Membership data 6 years post-graduation, welfare case files 3 years, financial records 7 years
5.5 Consent: Opt-in for marketing, legitimate interest for core membership services
5.6 Subject Access Requests: Processed within 30 calendar days, logged in SAR register

SECTION 6: COMPLAINTS & FEEDBACK

6.1 Student Complaints
- Stage 1: Informal resolution with relevant department (5 working days)
- Stage 2: Formal written complaint to Chief Executive (10 working days)
- Stage 3: Appeal to Board of Trustees sub-committee (20 working days)
- Stage 4: External review by OIA (Office of the Independent Adjudicator)

6.2 Feedback Mechanisms
- Annual Student Survey (November, target 25% response rate)
- Termly feedback forums (open to all students)
- Online feedback form on Guild website (monitored weekly)
- NSS free-text comments analysis (conducted by VP Education each July)
