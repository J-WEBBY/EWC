University of Oldbury Students' Guild — Commercial Performance Report
Period: September 2024 — August 2025
Prepared by: Nina Rodriguez, Commercial Director

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Total commercial revenue for 2024-25: £1,420,000 (up 8.2% on prior year)
Gross margin: 42.3% (target: 40%, prior year: 39.1%)
Net surplus from commercial operations: £185,000 (reinvested into student services)

The bar and events division drove the majority of growth, with Freshers' Week 2024 generating record revenue of £92,000 across 5 days. The Guild Shop underperformed due to increased competition from online retailers, partially offset by the new merchandise strategy launched in January.

DIVISION BREAKDOWN

1. BAR & HOSPITALITY
Revenue: £680,000 (prior year: £620,000, +9.7%)
Gross margin: 62% (industry benchmark: 65%)

Key metrics:
- Average weekly covers: 2,400 (term time)
- Most popular night: Friday "Indie Night" — avg 450 customers
- Cocktail sales up 34% following menu refresh in October
- Non-alcoholic options now 18% of drinks revenue (up from 9% — strategic push)
- Student staff hours: 8,200 across 45 casual employees

Challenges:
- Midweek trade still soft (Monday-Wednesday avg 120 covers)
- Staff turnover: 45% (high but typical for student workforce)
- Beer wastage: 4.2% (target: <3%, investigating line cleaning schedule)

Actions for 2025-26:
- Launch "Study & Sip" Wednesday afternoon concept (coffee bar transition 12-5pm)
- Partner with local brewery for exclusive Guild ale
- Install self-service beer taps to reduce queue times at peak events

2. EVENTS & ENTERTAINMENT
Revenue: £310,000 (prior year: £285,000, +8.8%)
Gross margin: 38%

Key events:
- Freshers' Week 2024: £92,000 revenue, 4,200 unique attendees across 12 events
- Summer Ball (June 2025): £68,000 revenue, 1,800 tickets sold at £38 each
- Monthly comedy nights: avg £4,500 per event, 85% capacity
- Society balls and formals: 28 events hosted, avg £3,200 each

Venue hire (external):
- 42 external bookings (conferences, weddings, community events)
- Revenue: £65,000
- Average booking value: £1,548
- Repeat booking rate: 60%

3. GUILD SHOP
Revenue: £280,000 (prior year: £295,000, -5.1%)
Gross margin: 28%

Analysis:
- Course materials: down 22% (students buying online/secondhand)
- Stationery: down 15%
- Guild merchandise: UP 45% — new branded range launched January 2025
- Snacks and convenience: UP 12% (benefiting from building footfall)
- Tech accessories: UP 8%

Strategic shift:
- Reducing shelf space for textbooks (move to print-on-demand partnership)
- Expanding merchandise range (hoodies, tote bags, water bottles with Guild branding)
- Exploring partnership with university for official graduation merchandise
- Testing vending machines in library and sports centre

4. CATERING & FOOD
Revenue: £150,000 (new division, launched January 2025)
Gross margin: 35%

Performance since launch:
- "Fuel" cafe (Guild ground floor): avg 180 transactions/day during term
- Menu: toasties, wraps, salads, coffee, smoothies
- Student satisfaction: 4.2/5 (internal survey, n=340)
- Vegan/vegetarian options: 40% of menu (student demand driven)

Targets for 2025-26:
- Break even by December 2025 (currently £8,000 behind target)
- Launch grab-and-go range for evening events
- Explore meal deal partnership with university catering

CAPITAL EXPENDITURE LOG
- New sound/lighting rig (Main Hall): £28,000 — approved BD-2024-004
- Cafe fit-out: £42,000 — funded from reserves
- EPOS system upgrade: £8,500 — improved stock management, ROI achieved in 4 months
- Draught beer system maintenance: £3,200

FINANCIAL PROJECTIONS 2025-26
Conservative: £1,480,000 (+4.2%)
Target: £1,560,000 (+9.9%)
Stretch: £1,620,000 (+14.1%)

Key growth drivers: Cafe reaching maturity, merchandise expansion, 3 additional external venue hires per month, new Wednesday trading concept.
