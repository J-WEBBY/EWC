University of Oldbury Students' Guild — Welfare & Advice Service Procedures
Confidential — Staff Use Only
Version 2.1 — Updated January 2026

OVERVIEW
The Guild Advice Service handles approximately 1,800 cases per academic year across five categories: academic appeals (35%), housing disputes (25%), financial hardship (20%), mental health support (12%), and employment rights (8%). This document outlines procedures for each.

SECTION 1: CASE MANAGEMENT

1.1 Initial Contact Protocol
- All enquiries logged in CaseTracker system within 1 working day
- Triage assessment completed using RAG rating:
  RED: Immediate risk (homelessness, suicidal ideation, violence) — same-day response
  AMBER: Urgent but not immediate (academic appeal deadline within 7 days, rent arrears) — 2-day response
  GREEN: Standard query — 5-day response
- Case reference assigned: WC-[YEAR]-[NUMBER] (e.g., WC-2026-0142)

1.2 Confidentiality
- All case data classified as CONFIDENTIAL
- Information shared only with: student's explicit consent, safeguarding duty, legal obligation
- Case files stored in encrypted database, access restricted to Advice team + VP Welfare
- Paper notes shredded at end of each day
- No case details discussed outside secure Advice office

1.3 Caseload Management
- Maximum active caseload per adviser: 40 cases
- Average case duration: Academic appeals 6 weeks, Housing 4 weeks, Financial 3 weeks
- Complex cases flagged to Senior Adviser for supervision
- Monthly case review meeting with VP Welfare

SECTION 2: ACADEMIC APPEALS

2.1 Grounds for Appeal (per university regulations)
- Procedural irregularity in assessment
- Extenuating circumstances not previously disclosed
- Evidence of bias or prejudice
- New evidence not available at time of original decision

2.2 Guild Adviser Role
- Review student's situation and assess strength of case (honest assessment even if unfavourable)
- Help student gather evidence (medical notes, personal statements, third-party testimony)
- Draft appeal letter (student must approve final version — their voice, not ours)
- Accompany student to formal hearings if requested
- Do NOT attend exam boards or access confidential university committee papers

2.3 Key Deadlines
- University appeal window: 21 calendar days from results publication
- Guild must submit within 14 days to allow university processing time
- Late submissions only accepted with evidence of extenuating circumstances

SECTION 3: HOUSING DISPUTES

3.1 Common Issues
- Deposit disputes (most common — 40% of housing cases)
- Disrepair and landlord non-compliance
- Housemate disputes and contract exit requests
- Rent increases mid-tenancy
- HMO licensing violations

3.2 Procedure
- Gather tenancy agreement, correspondence, photographic evidence
- Check if landlord is on Guild Accredited Landlord scheme (currently 45 properties, 180 beds)
- For deposit disputes: advise on TDS/MyDeposits adjudication process
- For disrepair: template letters provided (Letter-H1 through H5)
- Escalation: refer to Shelter, Citizens Advice, or Environmental Health as appropriate
- Do NOT provide legal advice — advisers signpost, not represent

3.3 Landlord Accreditation Scheme
- Accredited landlords agree to: fair deposits, 48hr repair response, no mid-year rent increases
- Annual inspection by Guild housing officer
- Removal from scheme: formal warning, then delisting after second substantiated complaint

SECTION 4: FINANCIAL HARDSHIP

4.1 Guild Hardship Fund
- Annual budget: £35,000 (university contribution £20,000, Guild contribution £15,000)
- Maximum grant per student: £500 per academic year
- Eligibility: enrolled UoO student, demonstrable financial need, all other funding explored first
- Application reviewed within 5 working days by Hardship Panel (Finance Director + VP Welfare + external trustee)
- Emergency payments (food/rent crisis): £100 max, authorised by VP Welfare same day

4.2 Assessment Criteria
- Priority given to: care leavers, estranged students, students with dependants, disabled students
- Evidence required: bank statements (3 months), income/expenditure breakdown, evidence of other applications (Student Finance, university hardship fund, bursaries)
- Funds paid directly to landlord/utility company where possible (safeguarding measure)

4.3 Money Advice
- Budget planning tools and templates available
- Referral pathway to StepChange for debt management
- Annual "Money Smart" campaign during November (financial literacy workshops)

SECTION 5: MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT

5.1 Important: Guild Does NOT Provide Clinical Services
- We provide: listening, signposting, practical support, advocacy
- We do NOT provide: counselling, therapy, diagnosis, medication advice
- Always refer to: university counselling service, GP, Samaritans (116 123), crisis team

5.2 Safeguarding Protocol
- If student discloses intent to harm self or others: IMMEDIATE escalation
- Contact: University Safeguarding Lead (Dr. Claire Simmons: 0121-XXX-XXXX)
- If imminent danger: call 999, then university security (ext. 2222)
- All safeguarding concerns logged in separate encrypted register
- Debrief for staff member within 24 hours with line manager

5.3 Ongoing Support
- "Check In" programme: fortnightly wellbeing drop-ins (no appointment needed)
- Exam stress support sessions: January and May
- Peer support training: 25 student volunteers trained per year
- Collaboration with Student Minds charity for awareness campaigns

SECTION 6: REPORTING

6.1 Monthly Report to VP Welfare
- Case volumes by category
- RAG status breakdown
- Average resolution time
- Emerging trends or policy issues
- Anonymised case studies for Board reporting

6.2 Annual Impact Report
- Published each July
- Total cases, outcomes, student satisfaction
- Policy changes achieved through casework evidence
- Presented to Board of Trustees and university senior management
